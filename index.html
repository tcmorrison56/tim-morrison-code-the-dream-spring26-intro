About

I came to the world of coding from a background in Product and Graphic design. I first developed a curiosity for web development when designing a website using a premade template website builder. I wanted to understand how everything worked under the surface, as well as have more freedom in the designing the site. From there I took a Udemy course by Jonas Schmedtmann on HTML and CSS fundamentals, followed by his course on JavaScript fundamentals. Now to gain a more complete understanding of the whole picture I am doing the fullstack development program from Code the Dream.

Experience

  • Drexel University B.S. of Product Design
    Graduated June 2017
    I earned my degree in Product Design from Drexel University, where I focused on the full process of bringing new ideas to life. The program gave me a solid foundation in everything from sketching and 3D modeling to hands-on prototyping and understanding how things actually get manufactured. It really shaped how I approach problem-solving, blending creative design with practical, real-world execution.
  • LaFrance Corp. Industrial Design Co-op
    March 2015 - September 2015
    I focused on bringing consumer product concepts to life through 3D CAD modeling. My job was to take the client's chosen materials and manufacturing methods—like molded plastics or formed metal—and make sure the 3D designs actually worked flawlessly within those constraints. I also put together the official blueprints for final reviews, set up a digital library of textures and materials to keep our team's renders consistent, and helped marketing map out our technical process for client pitches.
  • Freelance Design
    June 2017 - Present
    My role here was a mix of hands-on design and web development. On the hardware side, I used 3D modeling and 3D printing to test and build custom wire supports, making sure they wouldn't mess with antenna signals. On the digital side, I worked directly with clients to build custom features into their websites, writing clean code to get around platform limitations and making sure everything ran smoothly.
